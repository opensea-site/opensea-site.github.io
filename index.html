01 — Overview

What is the OpenSea site?

BLUF: OpenSea is a wallet-connected NFT marketplace for discovering, buying, selling, and creating NFTs across multiple blockchain ecosystems.

The OpenSea site is best understood as a broad NFT hub rather than a single collection storefront. Visitors can explore digital art, collectibles, gaming items, memberships, music, photography, drops, and creator pages, then connect a wallet when they want to transact. OpenSea also surfaces token and swap areas on its live homepage, but this overview focuses on the NFT workflows most users search for: browse, buy, sell, make offers, mint, and manage wallet-based collecting.

02 — How it works

How to use the OpenSea site

BLUF: Start at the verified opensea.io domain, connect a self-custody wallet, review the collection and network, then sign only the transactions you understand.

Using OpenSea is a wallet-based flow. You can browse without an account, but buying, making offers, listing, accepting offers, minting, and some creator tools require a compatible crypto wallet. Your wallet remains the control point for approvals and signatures, and network gas may apply depending on the chain and transaction. Before connecting, type or bookmark opensea.io, check the URL carefully, and avoid search ads, copied links, social DMs, and fake support accounts.

  1. 1
    Visit opensea.io and connect a wallet

    Go directly to opensea.io, confirm the domain, and connect a compatible self-custody wallet only when you are ready to act. Your wallet controls signatures, approvals, and assets, so do not share your seed phrase and do not sign prompts you do not understand.

  2. 2
    Browse and discover collections

    Use OpenSea discovery pages, search, categories, drops, and collection pages to compare items. Check the collection name, creator links, contract details, item history, network, and marketplace activity before treating a listing as legitimate.

  3. 3
    Buy or make offers

    For fixed-price items, review the item, network, total cost, and wallet prompt before buying. For offers, confirm the currency, expiration, collection or trait scope, and whether you need wrapped funds or gas on the relevant chain.

  4. 4
    List, sell, or mint

    If you own an NFT, you can list it or accept eligible offers through your wallet. Creators can create NFTs or collection drops where supported. Review marketplace fees, creator earnings, approvals, and gas before publishing or signing.

03 — Features

What you can do on OpenSea

BLUF: The OpenSea site brings discovery, trading, listing, minting, drops, and multichain NFT access into one marketplace experience.

Collectors use OpenSea to scan collections, compare floor prices, review item history, buy fixed-price NFTs, make offers, and follow drops. Sellers can list NFTs, accept offers, and manage collection pages. Creators can create NFTs and use OpenSea Studio-style tools for collections and drops where available. Because OpenSea support for chains, tokens, and creator tools changes over time, verify the exact network, wallet, and collection requirements on opensea.io before taking action.

Discover & browse

Explore NFT collections, drops, categories, activity, item pages, and creator profiles from a single marketplace interface.

Buy & offer

Buy listed NFTs or submit offers from a connected wallet after checking the item, chain, price, currency, and wallet confirmation.

Sell & list

List NFTs you own, adjust listings where available, accept offers, and review marketplace costs before signing seller transactions.

Mint / deploy

Create NFTs, build collections, and participate in drops through OpenSea creator tools when the relevant chain and collection setup are supported.

Multichain support

OpenSea supports many blockchain ecosystems, including major EVM networks and selected non-EVM functionality; always verify current chain support on OpenSea before transacting.

05 — Security

Using OpenSea safely

BLUF: The safest OpenSea habit is simple: verify opensea.io, protect your seed phrase, and treat every wallet signature as a transaction decision.

Phishing remains one of the most common NFT risks. Use the official OpenSea domain, avoid links from strangers, and never share your wallet seed phrase or secret recovery phrase. OpenSea says it will not ask for your wallet recovery phrase. Review token approvals periodically, revoke permissions you no longer need, and be skeptical of fake airdrops, surprise offers, impersonator emails, and anyone claiming you must send funds to fix a sale. If a wallet prompt is unclear, stop and investigate before signing.

06 — Fees

Fees & costs on OpenSea

BLUF: OpenSea costs can include marketplace fees, optional or enforced creator earnings, and blockchain gas, but the current model can change.

Do not assume an old OpenSea fee article, social post, or YouTube walkthrough is still accurate. OpenSea's fee and creator earnings model has changed over time, and the current rate can depend on transaction type, listing or offer settings, drop mechanics, and marketplace policy. Gas is paid to blockchain validators, not to OpenSea, and varies by network demand. Before buying, selling, minting, or accepting an offer, check the latest fee details and wallet confirmation directly on opensea.io and the OpenSea Help Center.

07 — FAQ

OpenSea FAQ

What is OpenSea?

OpenSea is an NFT marketplace where people can browse collections, buy listed NFTs, make offers, sell NFTs they own, and create or mint NFTs through supported creator tools. The OpenSea site also includes discovery, drops, activity, and wallet-connected transaction flows. Because it is a crypto marketplace, you use a compatible wallet for signatures and custody instead of a traditional username-and-password checkout.

Is the OpenSea site safe, and what is the real URL?

The real OpenSea URL is https://opensea.io. Safety depends heavily on user behavior: type or bookmark the official domain, avoid sponsored lookalikes, ignore support DMs, and never share your wallet seed phrase. OpenSea's own safety guidance says OpenSea will never ask for your wallet recovery phrase. Review wallet prompts carefully, limit token approvals, and stop if a transaction request is unclear.

How do I buy on OpenSea?

To buy on OpenSea, visit opensea.io, connect a compatible wallet, choose a collection and item, review the chain, price, currency, and item details, then approve the purchase in your wallet if everything matches. Some purchases may require the chain's native token for gas. If you make an offer instead of buying at a fixed price, check the offer amount, currency, expiration, and scope.

How do I sell on OpenSea?

To sell on OpenSea, connect the wallet that holds the NFT, open the item or collection workflow, choose listing terms, and review the marketplace fee, creator earnings, expiration, and any gas or approval prompts before signing. You can also accept eligible offers. Make sure the item is in your wallet, the collection is authentic, and the final wallet prompt matches the sale you intend.

What chains does OpenSea support?

OpenSea supports many blockchain ecosystems, and the list changes as networks and marketplace features evolve. Its 2026 support documentation references compatibility across Ethereum, Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism, Avalanche, Zora, Base, Blast, Sei, B3, Berachain, Flow, ApeChain, Soneium, Shape, Unichain, Ronin, Abstract, and others, with some network-specific limitations. Always check the current OpenSea Help Center and the collection page before assuming a chain is supported for NFTs.

Are there fees on OpenSea?

Yes, some OpenSea actions can involve costs. The main components to understand are marketplace fees, creator earnings or royalties, and blockchain gas. OpenSea's fee model and creator earnings rules have changed over time, so do not rely on outdated screenshots or old articles. Before buying, selling, minting, or accepting an offer, review the current fee details shown by OpenSea and your wallet confirmation.
